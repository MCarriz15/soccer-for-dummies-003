Italy

About Italian leagues

The Italian divisions have divided into four main division, with each one being more prestigious than the last. There have been a few alterations with them in these past few years. This is what they look like now.

Serie A

Serie A is the top professional football (soccer) league in Italy and is considered one of the most prestigious and competitive football leagues in the world. Serie A typically consists of 20 teams that compete against each other in a round-robin format. The top four teams qualify to play in the Champions League. The teams that finish the season in the bottom three positions are relegated to Serie B, the second tier of Italian football. Serie A is known for its tactical and defensive style of play, and the league is considered one of the most competitive in terms of defensive solidity. Teams earn three points for a win, one point for a draw, and no points for a loss. The league table is determined by points, and in case of a tie in points, tiebreakers such as goal difference and head-to-head results are used to rank teams.

Serie B

Serie B is the second-tier professional football league in Italy, sitting directly below Serie A. Serie B uses the same pointage system Serie A. It operates as a crucial part of the Italian football league system. Serie B typically consists of 20 teams that compete against each other in a round-robin format. The top two teams in the Serie B standings at the end of the season are automatically promoted to Serie A for the following season. Teams that finish the season from third to eighth place enter a playoff competition. The winner of the playoff is promoted to Serie A. The bottom three teams in the Serie B standings are relegated to Serie C, the third tier of Italian football.

Serie C

Serie C, officially known as Lega Pro, is the third-tier professional football league in Italy. Serie C is divided into multiple groups, and its structure underwent changes in the past. Serie C is typically divided into three groups: Serie C/A, Serie C/B, and Serie C/C. Each group consists of 20 teams, though the number can vary based on organizational decisions. The champions of each Serie C group are promoted directly to Serie B for the following season. Teams finishing in second place in each group might enter a playoff to determine additional promotions to Serie B. The number of teams promoted can vary based on league rules and regulations. The bottom teams in each Serie C group standings are relegated to Serie D, the fourth tier of Italian football.

Serie D

Serie D is the top level of non-professional football (amateur football) in Italy and serves as the fourth tier of the Italian football league system. This is where new teams can start a proffesional carrer and make a name for themselves. Serie D is divided into multiple regional groups, and the number of groups can vary. Each group typically consists of 18 to 20 teams. The champions of each Serie D group are usually promoted directly to Serie C for the following season. Teams finishing in second place in each group might enter a playoff to determine additional promotions to Serie C. The bottom teams in each Serie D group standings may face relegation to Eccellenza, which is the fifth tier of Italian football or they could also move down to regional divisions.
